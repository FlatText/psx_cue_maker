Autogenerate the missing CUE file for your PSX emulation bins.

Emulating games is great fun, but while SNES, NES and Genesis games are easy to find and run, PlayStation games are a bit more complicated. Unlike the others, they come in a dozen of different disc formats.

Most commonly, you'll find PSX games distributed as a zip-file and inside you'll find one or more bin files. However, in order for emulators or virtual drive managers to load your game correctly, you often need a CUE-file. If you attempt to load the first bin directly, it will often work, but background music will be missing.

By studying the cue file syntax, you can write a suitable cue file for your bins in notepad, but who wants to spend time fiddleling with that, when it can be automated?

Drag your bin files onto the dropzone below and have the cue sheet generated automatically. Your files will not be uploaded or anything. The dropzone is used to read the filenames of the bins, so this webpage can generate a cue sheet for you.
